About

A developer who ships, then keeps learning

I'm a Computer Science student who builds websites for a living and studies systems for fun. Client work with Global Web Daddy has given me hands-on experience shipping WordPress sites that real businesses depend on — from first layout to the plugin update six months later.

That work has pointed me toward the backend: how a site behaves under real traffic, how data moves between a form and a database, how a service stays up. Outside client projects, I build small tools in Flask, Django, and ASP.NET to keep that side of the craft sharp, and I treat every project — client or personal — as a chance to learn something I didn't know going in.
